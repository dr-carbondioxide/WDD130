everywhere i go
i see men
men, who are slimmer
men, who are richer
men, who are blessed
men, who work harder

and, everywhere i go
a part of me wishes i could be them
something from him,
the strength from him
the wisdom from him
the happiness from him

everywhere i go
i wish i was not myself
i wish i was kinder
i wish i listened
i wish i spoke
i wish i forgave quicker
i wish i could cry

i walk the roads in my neighbourhood late in the evening
the light from the vehicles headlights hit the back of my head from my eyes
i can hear myself struggling to let some light kiss my insides
i see kids my age, boys, who-
are better than me
i see them talk to girls in their evening tuition classes
i see the girls blush
and i wonder if i could be him
i wonder if i could attend those classes and be smarter
i wonder if im in need of a retired woman teaching me what i already know-
i wonder if i realise it is the truth, that i am not capable

i walk back home and i see myself walk towards me
he looks the same, no better
not happy
nor sad
nor taller
nor stronger
nor smarter,
as he reaches towards me he says

"everywhere i go,
i see you and i am reminded i am not enough"
